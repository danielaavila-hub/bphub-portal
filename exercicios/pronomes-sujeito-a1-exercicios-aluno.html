Subject Pronouns in Brazilian Portuguese

Subject pronouns tell you who is performing the action. Brazilian Portuguese often drops the pronoun in speech because the verb ending already shows the person — but using it is always correct and adds emphasis or clarity.

PessoaSingularPlural
1ª pessoa eu sou nós somos  /  a gente é
2ª pessoa você / tu / o senhor / a senhora é vocês são
3ª pessoa ele / ela é eles / elas são
▶ Você / vocês — 2nd person pronouns, but they take 3rd person verb forms.
▶ A gente — means "we" and is very common in everyday Brazilian Portuguese. It always takes a 3rd person singular verb: a gente é, not a gente somos.
▶ Tu — used in some regions of Brazil (North, Northeast, South). More informal than você.
▶ Vós — archaic. Avoid in Brazilian Portuguese.

Exercise 4

Free production

Write 5 sentences in Portuguese introducing yourself and people you know. Use 5 different subject pronouns — include a gente at least once.

Useful patterns:
Eu sou o/a _____.  ·  Eu sou _____ (profession / nationality).
