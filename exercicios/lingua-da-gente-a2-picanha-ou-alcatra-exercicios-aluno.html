Picanha ou Alcatra?

Two friends debate the best cut of Brazilian churrasco meat. Comparing preferences with gostar mais de, the hypothetical opinion pattern diria que, and the reassuring idiom não tem erro.

Read the dialogue

Two friends compare their favorite cuts of meat for a Brazilian barbecue.

A: Você gosta mais de picanha ou alcatra?

Do you like picanha or alcatra more?

B: Pessoalmente eu prefiro alcatra.

Personally I prefer alcatra.

A: Ah é, acho que a grande maioria diria que gosta mais de picanha.

Really, I believe most people would say that they like picanha more.

B: Pois é, eu tenho um gosto bem peculiar, não é?

Oh well, I have pretty peculiar tastes, right?

A: Sem dúvida. E que outros cortes você gosta?

No doubt. And what other cuts do you like?

B: Pra mim, bom, com a fraldinha não tem erro.

For me, well, with a fraldinha you can't go wrong.

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Gostar mais de X (ou Y)
English: to like X more, to prefer X (over Y)
To compare two preferences directly, use gostar mais de + noun: "Você gosta mais de picanha ou alcatra?" = "Do you like picanha or alcatra more?" It's a natural, everyday alternative to preferir.
Você gosta mais de picanha ou alcatra?
Do you like picanha or alcatra more? Eu gosto mais de carne do que de peixe.
I like meat more than fish.
Focus 2
Diria que... — condicional de dizer
English: would say that... (hypothetical opinion)
Diria (condicional of dizer) reports a hypothetical opinion or prediction — what someone would say, without stating it as fact: "a grande maioria diria que gosta mais de picanha" = "most people would say they like picanha more."
Acho que a maioria diria que prefere picanha.
I think most people would say they prefer picanha. Eu diria que ele tem razão.
I would say he's right.
Focus 3
Vocabulário do churrasco & "não tem erro"
Churrasco cuts of meat + a reassuring idiom
Não tem erro is a fixed expression meaning "you can't go wrong" — used to reassure someone that a choice is reliably good.
Picanha — top sirloin cap (Brazil's most famous churrasco cut)
Alcatra — rump steak
Fraldinha — flank steak
Corte — cut (of meat)
Churrasco — barbecue

Com a fraldinha, não tem erro.
With the fraldinha, you can't go wrong.
🥩 Culture note Brazilian churrasco is famous worldwide, especially for its seasoning with coarse sea salt and its distinctive cuts like picanha. But as this dialogue shows, not every Brazilian agrees that picanha is the best cut — tastes vary!
🔊 Phonology note Notice the nasal diphthong in não — it's produced through the nose, unlike English "now." Practice keeping the sound nasal all the way through, without a hard "n" at the end.
