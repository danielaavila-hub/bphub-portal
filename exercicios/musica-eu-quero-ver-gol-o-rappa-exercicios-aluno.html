Gramática em foco

Locução verbal — querer + infinitivo

Na música: "Eu quero ver gol"

Fórmula: verbo conjugado + infinitivo

A locução verbal junta dois verbos. O primeiro verbo é conjugado (muda com a pessoa). O segundo verbo fica no infinitivo (não muda).

Locução verbalEm inglêsExemplo
querer + infinitivo to want to Eu quero ver gol.
ir + infinitivo going to / will Eu vou torcer pro Brasil.
poder + infinitivo can / to be able to Você pode entrar!
precisar + infinitivo to need to Ele precisa ganhar.

Conjugação de querer — presente do indicativo

eu quero nós queremos você / ele / ela quer vocês / eles / elas querem
Contexto cultural
🎤

O Rappa

O Rappa is a Rio de Janeiro band formed in 1993. They mix funk, hip-hop, reggae, and rock to tell stories about daily life in Rio's streets and communities. "Eu Quero Ver Gol" is from their breakthrough album Rappa Mundi (1996) — one of the most celebrated Brazilian hip-hop albums of the 1990s.

⚽

Football in Brazil

Football is the most popular sport in Brazil — many Brazilians call it a paixão nacional (the national passion). Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup 5 times. On match day, cities grow quiet, streets fill with fans in yellow jerseys, and everyone watches o jogo together.

🏆

Gol de placa

A gol de placa (literally: a goal worthy of a commemorative plaque) is something truly extraordinary. The phrase was born after Pelé scored an incredible goal at Maracanã in 1961. Journalists said it deserved a commemorative plaque — and one was actually installed! Today Brazilians use it for anything amazing: "Essa comida foi um gol de placa!"

🌊

A vida carioca

This song paints a vivid picture of life in Rio de Janeiro (carioca = from Rio). The singer takes a crowded bus to the beach, cheers for his team, and dreams of seeing a goal. It captures the energy, the slang, and the everyday rhythm of carioca street life.

Vocabulário carioca
Palavra / expressão Significado (English) Na música…
gol de placa a spectacular goal; anything extraordinary "Não precisa ser de placa" — any goal is fine!
morro hill; in Rio, a hillside community (favela) "no pé do morro" = at the base of the hill
bonde originally a tram; in Rio slang, a crew, a group of friends "Méier-Copacabana é o bonde ideal"
rebu confusion, chaos, commotion "No ponto final o rebu é total"
rango food (very informal carioca slang) "Tô no rango desde as 2…"
geral the cheap standing section of a stadium; also: everyone / everything "Tô com a geral no bolso" = I've got my standing ticket
torcer to cheer, to support a football team "Vou torcer… pro meu time ganhar"
xingar to curse, to swear; in football, to yell at players or the referee (very common!) "Vou torcer, vou xingar pro meu time ganhar"
