Eu Quero Ver Gol

O Rappa · álbum Rappa Mundi, 1996

"Eu Quero Ver Gol" is one of the most fun and energetic songs by O Rappa, a Rio de Janeiro band known for mixing hip-hop, funk, and reggae. Released in 1996, the song follows a carioca fan on his way to watch a football match — from the crowded bus to the beach, all the way to the stadium. The language is full of Rio slang, street life, and pure passion for football.

Contexto cultural

🎤 O Rappa
O Rappa is a Rio de Janeiro band formed in 1993. They are known for mixing funk, hip-hop, reggae, and rock to tell stories about daily life in Rio's streets and favela communities. "Eu Quero Ver Gol" is from their breakthrough album Rappa Mundi (1996) — one of the most celebrated Brazilian hip-hop albums of the decade.
⚽ Football in Brazil
Football is the most popular sport in Brazil — many Brazilians call it a paixão nacional (the national passion). Brazil has won the FIFA World Cup 5 times. On match day, cities grow quiet, streets fill with fans wearing the yellow jersey (a camisa amarela), and everyone watches o jogo together — at home, in bars, or at the stadium.
🏆 Gol de placa
A gol de placa literally means "a goal worthy of a commemorative plaque." The phrase was born after Pelé scored an incredible goal against Fluminense at Maracanã in 1961. Journalists wrote it deserved a plaque — and one was actually installed at the stadium! Today, Brazilians use "gol de placa" for anything extraordinary, not just in football: "Essa comida foi um gol de placa!" (That food was incredible!)
🌊 A vida carioca
Carioca means someone from Rio de Janeiro. This song paints a vivid picture of everyday Rio life: taking a crowded bus (lotação), going to the beach, buying food from street vendors (lemon, iced tea, sliced watermelon), and watching football with passion. The song captures the energy, the slang, and the rhythm that make carioca street culture unique.

Gramática

Locução verbal — querer + infinitivo

Na música:
Eu quero ver gol.
Vou torcer, vou xingar pro meu time ganhar.
A locução verbal (verbal phrase) joins two verbs. The first verb is conjugated (changes with the person). The second verb stays in the infinitive (never changes).

Fórmula: verbo conjugado + infinitivo
Locução verbalMeaningExemplo
querer + inf.to want toEu quero ver gol.
ir + inf.going to / willEu vou torcer pro Brasil.
poder + inf.can / to be able toVocê pode entrar!
precisar + inf.to need toEle precisa ganhar.

Conjugação de querer — presente do indicativo:
eu quero nós queremos você / ele / ela quer vocês / eles / elas querem
