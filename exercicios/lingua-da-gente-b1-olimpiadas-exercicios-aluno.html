Você Vai Assistir a Cerimônia de Abertura?

Two friends get excited about the Olympic opening ceremony and the torch relay passing through Brazil — a short, high-energy dialogue built entirely on the pretérito perfeito for talking about what you've seen and done.

Língua da Gente · Intermediate 09

Diálogo · Bilingual transcript

A Você vai assistir a cerimônia de abertura? Are you going to watch the opening ceremonies?
B Claro, adoro ver o momento quando todos os atletas entram no estádio. Of course, I love to see the moment when all of the athletes enter the stadium.
A Eu acho legal ver quando entram com a chama da tocha olímpica. I think it's cool when they enter with the flame of the Olympic torch.
B Você acompanhou a rota do revezamento da tocha? Have you followed the route of the torch relay?
A Acompanhei, até assisti em Santos quando os condutores passaram. I have, I was even in Santos when the torch bearers passed by.
Foco Gramatical

Pretérito Perfeito — experiências e ações concluídas

This short dialogue is built almost entirely on the pretérito perfeito, the tense for completed past actions and experiences: "Você acompanhou a rota do revezamento da tocha?" / "Acompanhei, até assisti em Santos quando os condutores passaram."

StructureExample from the dialogue
pergunta no perfeito Você acompanhou a rota da tocha?
resposta + até (adiciona uma informação surpreendente) Acompanhei, até assisti em Santos.
quando + perfeito (ação pontual, no passado) ...quando os condutores passaram.

How to form it: regular verbs take -ei/-ou (falar, entrar) or -i/-iu (assistir, partir) in the eu/ele forms used here.

-AR verbs (eu / ele-ela)
acompanhar → acompanhei / acompanhou
entrar → entraram
passar → passaram
-IR verbs (eu)
assistir → assisti
Vocabulário & Cultura

Vocabulário dos Jogos Olímpicos

This episode was recorded in the lead-up to the Rio 2016 Olympics, when the torch relay travelled through Brazilian cities — including Santos, mentioned in the dialogue. Key vocabulary:

PalavraO que éExemplo do diálogo
cerimônia de abertura opening ceremony você vai assistir a cerimônia de abertura?
atletas / estádio athletes / stadium todos os atletas entram no estádio
tocha / chama olímpica torch / Olympic flame a chama da tocha olímpica
revezamento da tocha torch relay a rota do revezamento da tocha
💬 Santos — a coastal city in the state of São Paulo, home to football legend Pelé and one of Brazil's busiest ports. Like many Brazilian cities, it hosted a leg of the Olympic torch relay before the Rio 2016 Games.
Foco Fonológico

Ligação entre palavras — "os atletas", "quando os"

In connected speech, Brazilians link a word-final -s to the vowel that starts the next word. "Os atletas" is pronounced as one flowing unit — "o-za-tle-tas" — not "os" + "atletas" separately. The same happens in "quando os condutores" → "quan-do-zos-con-du-to-res."

This liaison (in Portuguese, sandhi) is one of the biggest reasons spoken Brazilian Portuguese sounds faster and more "joined together" than the written word suggests — train your ear for it while listening to the episode again.

Exercise 3

What do they mean?

Choose the best meaning or explanation for each expression from the dialogue.

1. "Claro", as an answer here, means:
a"It's clear/transparent" — describing an object.
b"Of course!" — an enthusiastic yes.
c"I'm not sure" — hesitation.
2. "Eu acho legal" is closest in meaning to:
a"I think it's illegal."
b"I think it's cool / nice."
c"I think it's legal, as in permitted by law."
3. In "você acompanhou a rota da tocha?", "acompanhar" means:
aTo physically walk beside someone the whole way.
bTo follow (news, a route, an event) — not necessarily in person.
cTo organize or plan an event.
4. "Acompanhei, até assisti em Santos..." — what does "até" add here?
aIt marks a time limit — "until."
bIt adds a surprising extra detail — "I even..."
cIt signals doubt about what follows.
