Read the dialogue

Two friends read and react to a new blog post describing an unpleasant acquaintance.

A: Você leu o novo post da Mara no blog dela?

Did you read Mara's new blog post?

B: Não, o que ela escreveu?

No, what did she write?

A: Ela disse que a colega de trabalho dela é muito chata e metida.

She said her coworker is very annoying and full of herself.

B: Que é isso! Ela não pode falar assim de uma colega no blog!

No way! She can't talk like that about a coworker on her blog!

A: Eu sei, mas ela disse que se a colega fosse mais gente boa, ela nem teria escrito nada.

I know, but she said if her coworker were nicer, she wouldn't even have written anything.

B: Bom, se fosse eu, eu ia conversar com ela pessoalmente, não colocar no blog.

Well, if it were me, I'd talk to her in person, not put it on the blog.

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Que é isso! — No way! / Come on! (exclamation of disagreement)
English: no way! / come on! (exclamation expressing disapproval or disbelief)
Que é isso! is a common exclamation used to react with surprise or disapproval to something someone said or did.
Que é isso! Ela não pode falar assim!
No way! She can't talk like that!Que é isso, não precisa se desculpar.
Come on, no need to apologize.
Focus 2
Se + imperfeito do subjuntivo — Hypothetical "if" clauses
English: if + imperfect subjunctive (hypothetical situations)
Se + imperfeito do subjuntivo describes a hypothetical or contrary-to-fact situation, often paired with the conditional (seria, ia) in the main clause.
Se a colega fosse mais gente boa, ela nem teria escrito nada.
If her coworker were nicer, she wouldn't even have written anything.Se fosse eu, eu ia conversar com ela.
If it were me, I'd talk to her.
Focus 3
Adjetivos de personalidade — Personality adjectives
English: personality adjectives
This dialogue introduces common (and sometimes unflattering) personality adjectives, useful for describing people in everyday conversation.
Chata/o — annoying
Metida/o — full of oneself, stuck-up
Gente boa — a good person, nice
💬 Blogs pessoais Personal blogs were a popular way for Brazilians to vent and share opinions in the 2000s-2010s — often sparking exactly this kind of debate about what's appropriate to post publicly.
🔊 Metida Metida (feminine) / metido (masculine) — from the verb meter (to put/stick), colloquially meaning someone who thinks too highly of themselves.
