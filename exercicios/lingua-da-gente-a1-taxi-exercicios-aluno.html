💬 Dialogue

A gente chama um táxi?
Read through the dialogue. Then listen again and follow along.
A
A gente chama um táxi?
Should we call a taxi?
B
Não, eu prefiro andar.
No, I'd rather walk.
A
Mas é muito longe.
But it's really far.
B
Tá bom, pode chamar então.
OK, you can call one then.
📌 "A gente" = nós (informal "we")
In everyday Brazilian Portuguese, a gente is used far more than nós to mean "we." It always takes a 3rd person singular verb (the same as ele/ela):
PronounFormalInformal (spoken)
we callnós chamamosa gente chama
we gonós vamosa gente vai
we prefernós preferimosa gente prefere
we arenós somos / estamosa gente é / tá
A gente chama um táxi? — literally "the people call a taxi?" but means "Should we call a taxi?" The question intonation makes it a suggestion.
📌 PREFERIR + infinitive  ·  PODER + infinitive
StructureExampleMeaning
preferir + infEu prefiro andar.I prefer to walk / I'd rather walk
preferir + infEla prefere ficar em casa.She'd rather stay home
poder + infPode chamar então.You can call / Go ahead and call
poder + infPode falar.You can speak / Go ahead
Pode + infinitive is also used to give permission or encourage someone to proceed — very natural and polite.
🗣️ Ponto fonológico — "Tá bom": Just like estou → tô (EP7), the verb está contracts to tá in informal speech. So Está bom → Tá bom. You'll also hear tá alone as a quick "OK" or "got it" — like a verbal nod. This reduction of est- to just a t is one of the most consistent features of colloquial Brazilian Portuguese.
🚕 Transporte no Brasil: Taxis are common in Brazilian cities, but since around 2015 Uber (and 99, its local competitor) have become the default choice for most urban Brazilians — cheaper and easier to track. Walking distances are often underestimated by visitors, as Brazilian cities are large and the heat can be intense. Muito longe (very far) is a common warning locals give before sending someone on foot!

Exercise 1 — True or False?
Based on the dialogue, choose the correct answer.

1. Person A suggests calling a taxi.

2. Person B prefers to take a taxi.

3. Person A says the destination is very far.

4. In the end, they decide to walk.

Exercise 4 — Your turn
Answer in Portuguese.
Write your own answers. There are no right or wrong responses — this activity is about expressing your own ideas and experiences.

1. How do you prefer to get around in your city? Answer using Eu prefiro + [transport].

2. Write a short dialogue (3–4 lines) between two people deciding how to get somewhere. Use A gente + verb? for the suggestion and Prefiro / Tá bom in the replies.

3. Use Pode + infinitive to give permission or encourage someone in two different situations from your work or daily life.

After completing the exercise, send it to your teacher so she can provide careful feedback and help you improve your Portuguese.
