Step 2 — Read & Listen Again

📄 The dialogue
Second listen: Follow the transcript below while you listen. Pay attention to the highlighted words.
A: Você vem de onde, Paulo?
Where do you come from, Paulo?
B: Aqui do Rio, e você?
Here in Rio, and you?
A: Eu sou de Brasília.
I am from Brasília.
B: Dizem que o céu lá é enorme, não é?
They say the sky there is huge, right?
🌎 Culture note: Brasília is famous for its wide-open skies — the city was planned in the 1950s by urban designer Lúcio Costa and architect Oscar Niemeyer in a flat region (o cerrado), which gives it a famously enormous sky.

Step 3 — Language Focus

📌 Ser de vs. Vir de — Expressing origin

In the dialogue, the two characters use two different ways to ask and talk about where they are from. Both are common in Brazilian Portuguese:

Structure Example Meaning
ser de + place Eu sou de Brasília. I am from Brasília. (origin/identity)
vir de + place Você vem de onde? Where do you come from? (slightly more conversational)
ser + de + article Sou do Rio. / Sou da Bahia. I'm from Rio / Bahia. (de + o = do; de + a = da)
Quick question forms:
Você é de onde? — Where are you from?
Você vem de onde? — Where do you come from?
Both are correct and interchangeable in everyday speech.

Tag question: não é?

In English, tag questions change form: "it's big, isn't it?" / "they're nice, aren't they?"
In Brazilian Portuguese, não é? (or informal né?) works for all situations:

O céu lá é enorme, não é? — The sky there is huge, right?
Você é do Brasil, né? — You're from Brazil, right?
