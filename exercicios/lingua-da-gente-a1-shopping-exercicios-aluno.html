🇧🇷 Culture note — O shopping
Brazilians call shopping malls o shopping — a borrowed English word that became fully Portuguese, complete with its own article. You'll hear: "Vou ao shopping." (I'm going to the mall.) Brazilian shoppings are large, air-conditioned, and a major social space — especially in big cities like São Paulo and Rio.
🔊 Phonology Note — "ch" = SH In Brazilian Portuguese, ch is always pronounced like the English sh — never like "ch" in "cheese." So chego sounds like shego, and chá (tea) sounds like shá. This is consistent throughout Brazilian Portuguese. Practice: che-go, cha-mar, cho-co-la-te.
