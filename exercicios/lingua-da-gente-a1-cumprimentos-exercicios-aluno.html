Step 3 — Language Focus

📖 Greetings in Brazilian Portuguese

There are many ways to ask "how are you?" in Brazil — and they all work in informal conversation.

Greeting (how to ask) Response Register
Tudo bem? Tudo bem! / Tudo bom! / Tudo! informal, very common
Tudo bom? Tudo bom! / Tudo! informal, very common
Como vai você? Vou bem, obrigado/a! slightly more formal
Como está? Estou bem, e você? neutral / semi-formal
Também (me too) Também! used to mirror the other person's answer
💡 "Né?" — the Brazilian tag question

You already heard né? in episode 01. Here it appears again: "Melhor agora, né?" It's used to invite the other person to agree with you. English equivalent: "...right?" or "...isn't it?"

Que + adjective = an exclamation of feeling:
Que ótimo! — How great! / That's great!
Que bom! — How nice! / Great!
Que legal! — How cool! / Awesome!

🔊 Phonology Note — Nasal vowels Brazilian Portuguese has nasal vowels that don't exist in English. Listen carefully to bem and também — the final m doesn't close your lips fully. Instead, it nasalizes the vowel. Think of "beng" but with a very soft, open ending. The more you listen, the more natural it sounds!
