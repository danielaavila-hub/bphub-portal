Read the dialogue

A customer arrives at her usual lanchonete. Carlinhos, the waiter, is happy to see her.

A: Ô Carlinhos, qual é o prato do dia?

B: Hoje tem bife à milanesa com arroz e feijão.

A: É? Como é? É bom?

B: É muito bom, você quer experimentar?

A: Obrigada, prefiro o mesmo de sempre.

B: X-Bacon, batatas fritas, e um guaraná bem gelado, não é?

A: É isso mesmo.

A: Hey Carlinhos, what is the daily special?

B: Today is breaded steak with rice and beans.

A: Yeah? How is it? Is it good?

B: It's really good — do you want to try it?

A: Thanks, I prefer the same as always.

B: Cheese bacon burger, french fries, and a super cold guaraná, right?

A: You've got it.

Language focus
Focus 1
Preferir + substantivo
English: to prefer (something)
Preferir is a regular -ir verb with a vowel shift in the stem: the e becomes i in all forms except nós and vós. It's used followed by a noun or an infinitive.
Eu prefiro o mesmo de sempre.
I prefer the same as always. Você prefere arroz ou batatas?
Do you prefer rice or potatoes? Ela prefere comer em casa.
She prefers to eat at home. Nós preferimos o prato do dia.
We prefer the daily special.
Focus 2
Querer + infinitivo — offers and invitations
English: Do you want to…? / Would you like to…?
Querer + infinitivo is one of the most natural ways to offer or invite someone to try something. It's direct and friendly — much more common than the more formal gostaria de in everyday conversation.
Você quer experimentar?
Do you want to try it? Você quer tomar alguma coisa?
Do you want something to drink? Você quer ver o cardápio?
Do you want to see the menu? Você quer pedir agora?
Do you want to order now?
Focus 3
Expressões e vocabulário do diálogo
Expressions & food vocabulary
These expressions and food words appear constantly in Brazilian lanchonetes, restaurantes, and everyday conversation. Learn them as ready-made chunks.
O mesmo de sempre — the usual / same as always
É isso mesmo — That's right / You've got it / Exactly
Não é? — Right? / Isn't it? (tag question, very common)
Como é? — What's it like? / How is it?
Bem gelado/a — super cold / ice cold

Bife à milanesa — breaded steak
Arroz e feijão — rice and beans (the Brazilian staple)
X-Bacon — cheese bacon burger (X = queijo, from "cheese")
Batatas fritas — french fries
Guaraná — guaraná soft drink (iconic Brazilian brand: Guaraná Antarctica)
🍽 Culture note Arroz e feijão is more than a dish — it's the backbone of the Brazilian daily meal. Most Brazilians eat it for lunch every day, often with a bife, salad, and farofa. The expression "arroz e feijão" also means something simple and reliable: "É arroz e feijão" = "It's nothing fancy."
🔊 Phonology note The X in X-Bacon and X-Burguer is pronounced like the English "sh" — "shees" from "cheese". This is a fun example of how Brazilian Portuguese adapted English food vocabulary. You'll hear X-Salada, X-Tudo (the works!) at any lanchonete.
