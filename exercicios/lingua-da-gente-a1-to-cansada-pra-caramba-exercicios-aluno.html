Eduardo, vamos dormir, tô cansada pra caramba.

B
Mas ainda é cedo.

Eu sei, mas estou com sono.

B
Tá bom, já tô indo.
OK, I'm coming.

Step 3 — Language Focus

📖 Estar com + noun — Physical states

A says "estou com sono" — Portuguese uses estar com + noun (not an adjective!) for many physical states, unlike English "to be sleepy/hungry/thirsty."

Expression English
estar com sono to be sleepy
estar com fome to be hungry
estar com sede to be thirsty
estar com calor/frio to be hot/cold
📖 Pra caramba — A very common intensifier

A says she's tired "pra caramba" — a casual, mild-swear-adjacent intensifier meaning "a lot / really," used constantly in informal Brazilian speech.

Phrase Meaning Example
pra caramba a whole lot / really Tô cansada pra caramba.
já tô indo I'm coming / on my way Resposta comum quando alguém chama.
💤 Tô = Estou In very casual speech, estou is almost always shortened to tô — you'll hear this constantly in real conversations, though it's not used in formal writing.
