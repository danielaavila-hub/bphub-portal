Não Gosto de Açaí

A friend just can't get on board with Brazil's favorite berry. The disbelief expression como que...?, the intensifier nem ("not even"), and a classic Brazilian saying about taste.

Read the dialogue

A friend offers açaí, and is stunned to hear the answer.

A: Que tal um açaí?

How about some açaí?

B: Obrigado, eu não gosto de açaí.

Thanks, I don't like açaí.

A: Como que não gosta, todo mundo gosta de açaí.

What do you mean you don't like it, everyone likes açaí.

B: Ah é? Bom, pra mim ele tem um gosto meio estranho.

Oh yeah? Well, for me it has kind of a strange taste.

A: Nem com banana e granola?

Not even with banana and granola?

B: Não, nem assim. Como dizem, "gosto não se discute."

No, not even that way. As they say, "you can't argue about taste."

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Como que...? — expressando descrença
English: What do you mean...? / How come...?
Como que at the start of a question expresses surprise or disbelief at what someone just said: "Como que não gosta?" = "What do you mean you don't like it?" It's a very natural, everyday way to push back on a statement.
Como que não gosta, todo mundo gosta de açaí!
What do you mean you don't like it, everyone likes açaí! Como que você esqueceu da reunião?
How could you forget about the meeting?
Focus 2
Nem — "not even"
English: not even
Nem intensifies a negation, ruling out even the most likely exception: "Nem com banana e granola?" = "Not even with banana and granola?" — "Não, nem assim" = "No, not even like that."
Nem com banana e granola?
Not even with banana and granola? Ele nem respondeu quando eu perguntei.
He didn't even answer when I asked.
Focus 3
Vocabulário do açaí & um ditado popular
Açaí vocabulary + a classic Brazilian saying
Key vocabulary from the dialogue, plus a fixed expression Brazilians use constantly when a taste disagreement can't be resolved.
Açaí — açaí (Amazonian berry, usually served as a frozen smoothie/bowl)
Granola — granola
Gosto — taste
Estranho — strange
Todo mundo — everyone

"Gosto não se discute."
Literally "taste isn't argued about" — the Brazilian equivalent of "there's no accounting for taste."
🫐 Culture note Açaí is a purple Amazonian berry, usually blended into a thick, frozen smoothie and served in a bowl topped with granola, banana, and sometimes condensed milk. It's a nationwide favorite in Brazil — but the taste is famously divisive for first-timers.
🔊 Phonology note Açaí is stressed on the last syllable: a-ça-Í. The final "í" carries an accent mark and should be pronounced clearly and openly, not swallowed.
