Language Note

The relative pronoun QUE in Brazilian Portuguese

📌 What is QUE?

In English, we use words like "who," "that," or "which" to connect two ideas about the same person or thing. In Portuguese, the most common word is QUE — used for people, objects, and places.

QUE recovers a noun already mentioned and connects it to a new idea — letting you say more in one sentence instead of two:

Eu li um livro. + Esse livro era interessante.
= Eu li um livro QUE era interessante.

English Portuguese
the city that I love a cidade que eu amo
the teacher who explains well o professor que explica bem
the recipe that my mom makes a receita que minha mãe faz
Key point: In Portuguese, QUE works for people, things, and places — no need to choose between "who," "that," or "which." It's always que.
