Read the dialogue

A parent asks their child about homework, piano practice, and the cat — getting the same answer every time.

A: Você fez o dever de casa?

Did you do your homework?

B: Ainda não.

Not yet.

A: E o piano, você praticou alguma coisa?

And the piano, did you practice it at all?

B: Ainda não.

Not yet.

A: E o gato, você deu alguma coisa para ele comer?

And the cat, did you give the cat any food?

B: Ainda não.

Not yet..

A: O que você estava fazendo esse tempo todo?

What have you been doing all this time?

B: Nada, quer dizer, assistindo a televisão.

Nothing, that is, watching television.

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Imperfeito contínuo — Estar (imperfeito) + gerúndio
English: was doing (ongoing action in the past)
Estava fazendo (imperfeito of estar + gerúndio) describes an ongoing action in the past, perfect for asking what someone has been up to over a stretch of time.
O que você estava fazendo esse tempo todo?
What have you been doing all this time?Eu estava assistindo televisão.
I was watching television.
Focus 2
Ainda não — Not yet (review)
English: not yet (reinforcing this pattern from earlier episodes)
Ainda não repeated three times in a row builds comedic tension — a great illustration of how useful and flexible this simple phrase is.
Você fez o dever de casa? Ainda não.
Did you do your homework? Not yet.
Focus 3
Quer dizer — That is to say / I mean
English: that is (to say) / I mean (self-correction)
Quer dizer is used to clarify or correct what you just said — very natural when catching yourself mid-sentence.
Nada, quer dizer, assistindo a televisão.
Nothing, I mean, watching television.Ele chegou tarde, quer dizer, não chegou ainda.
He arrived late, I mean, he hasn't arrived yet.
📺 Procrastinação universal This dialogue captures a universally relatable parent-child moment — the classic pattern of putting off homework, chores, and responsibilities in favor of watching TV!
🔊 Estava fazendo Note the two-part structure: estava (imperfeito of estar) + gerúndio (fazendo) — both parts are needed to express an ongoing past action.
