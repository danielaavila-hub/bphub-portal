Step 3 — Language Focus

📖 Asking for Directions

The key structure is simple: Onde é o/a ___? — "Where is the ___?" Add com licença to be polite when approaching a stranger.

PortugueseEnglishNotes
Com licençaExcuse meto get someone's attention
Onde é o banheiro?Where is the bathroom?Onde é o/a + place
Lá atrásBack thereindicating distance + direction
À esquerdaOn the leftà = a + a (contraction)
À direitaOn the rightà = a + a (contraction)
A primeira / segunda portaThe first / second doorordinal numbers agree in gender
Obrigado / ObrigadaThank you-o if you're male, -a if you're female
Com licença vs. Desculpa — what's the difference?
Com licença = excuse me (asking permission to pass or interrupt) — before the action.
Desculpa / Desculpe = sorry (apologizing for a mistake) — after the action.

In the dialogue, A says com licença to approach B politely. B says desculpa when correcting the wrong directions.
🔊 Phonology Note — The "nh" sound The word banheiro contains the "nh" combination — a nasal palatal sound. Think of the "ny" in "canyon," the Spanish "ñ," or the French "gn" in "champagne." It's softer and more nasal than a regular N. Practice: ba-NHEI-ro. The stress falls on the second syllable.
