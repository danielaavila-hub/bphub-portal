Read the dialogue

Two friends talk about wrinkles and whether plastic surgery would really be worth it.

A: Eu acho que não faria uma cirurgia plástica, mas se fizesse, que parte mudaria?

I'm not saying I would do plastic surgery, but if I did, what part should I change?

B: Mas que pergunta é essa! Porquê você pergunta isso?

What kind of question is that! Why would you ask that?

A: São essas rugas no rosto, são bem feias, viu?

It's these wrinkles in my face, they are so ugly, you know?

B: Mas quem faz a cirurgia plástica acaba parecendo super artificial.

But those that have plastic surgery end up looking really fake.

A: Mesmo assim, é duro aceitar essas rugas.

Still, it's hard to accept these wrinkles.

B: Querida, há uma beleza que só vem com o passar do tempo, você está linda.

Dear, there is beauty that only comes with the years, you are beautiful.

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Se + imperfeito do subjuntivo + condicional — Hypotheticals
English: if I did (hypothetical), I would change...
Se fizesse (imperfeito do subjuntivo) + mudaria (condicional) is the classic pattern for imagining a hypothetical, contrary-to-fact situation — "if I did X, I would Y."
Se fizesse, que parte mudaria?
If I did, what part would I change?Se eu tivesse tempo, viajaria mais.
If I had time, I would travel more.
Focus 2
Mesmo assim — Even so / still
English: even so, still (concession)
Mesmo assim introduces a concession — acknowledging a counterpoint but maintaining your original feeling.
Mesmo assim, é duro aceitar essas rugas.
Even so, it's hard to accept these wrinkles.Ele estudou bastante, mesmo assim não passou.
He studied a lot, but still didn't pass.
Focus 3
Beleza e envelhecimento — Aging gracefully
English: vocabulary and expressions about beauty and aging
This dialogue offers a gentle, culturally resonant way of talking about accepting the signs of aging.
Rugas — wrinkles
Há uma beleza que só vem com o passar do tempo.
There is a beauty that only comes with time.
💬 Você está linda Reassuring someone about their appearance with warmth (você está linda) is a very natural, common gesture of affection in Brazilian conversation, especially between close friends and partners.
🔊 Fizesse / Mudaria Note the two different verb forms working together: fizesse (imperfeito do subjuntivo, for the "if" clause) and mudaria (condicional, for the result) — this pairing is essential for hypotheticals.
