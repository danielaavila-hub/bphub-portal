Os Três Verbos — Regras e Contextos

→
levar
to take (away from here, towards there)

Levar = movement away from the speaker towards another place. Think of it as "take away." The speaker is not at the destination.

  • Você pode levar a mamãe na consulta médica?Can you take mom to the doctor's appointment? (you are here, the clinic is elsewhere)
  • Alguém pode levar os guardanapos?Can someone take the napkins? (Lucas is at his house — they go away from him, to the party)
  • Ela levou a vovó para a festa.She took grandma to the party. (from grandma's place → party)
  • Não leva guarda-chuva — não vai chover.Don't take an umbrella — it's not going to rain.
  • O time levou o troféu pra casa.The team took the trophy home.
←
trazer
to bring (towards the speaker, towards here)

Trazer = movement towards the speaker's location. Think of it as "bring here." The speaker is at the destination, or is asking someone to come to them.

  • Bê, você traz a salada de frutas?Bê, will you bring the fruit salad? (Marcos speaks from the party house — movement towards him)
  • Não esquece de trazer o presente!Don't forget to bring the present! (Ana is at the party house — movement towards her)
  • Trouxe presentes do Brasil.I brought presents from Brazil. (brought here, to where you are now)
  • Pode trazer a conta?Can you bring the bill? (at a restaurant — the waiter comes to you)
  • Traz aquele queijo que a vovó adora.Bring that cheese grandma loves. (said from the party house — Beatriz, at her own home, answers: "Levo sim!")
✋
pegar
to grab / pick up / get / take / catch

Pegar is not about direction — it's about obtaining, grabbing, or picking something up. It has several uses that English covers with different verbs.

  • Vou pegar o bolo na confeitaria.I'll pick up the cake at the bakery. (go get it)
  • Peguei os refrigerantes no mercado.I got / picked up the sodas at the supermarket.
  • Pego o gelo na hora.I'll grab the ice on the way / at the time.
  • Pega as chaves na gaveta.Grab the keys from the drawer.
  • Vou pegar um Uber.I'll take / get an Uber. (use transportation)
  • Pega o ônibus na esquina.Take the bus at the corner. (board transportation)
  • Peguei um resfriado.I caught a cold. (contract an illness)
  • Você pode pegar as crianças na escola?Can you pick the kids up from school?

Atenção — Erros Comuns

⚠ Cuidado
  • "Bring" is not always trazer. When you take something away from where you are, use levar: "I'll bring my car" (= I'll bring it to you) → Vou levar o meu carro (not trazer, if you're driving away to meet someone).
  • "Take" is not always levar. "Take the bus" → pegar o ônibus (not levar — you're boarding, not transporting the bus).
  • "Get" is almost always pegar. "Get a taxi," "get the groceries," "get a cold" → pegar um táxi, pegar as compras, pegar um resfriado.
  • Levar vs. trazer depends on YOUR position. If you're at the party asking someone to come: traz guardanapos. If you're at home about to leave: levo guardanapos.
