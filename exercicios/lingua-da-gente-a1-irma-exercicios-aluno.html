💬 Dialogue

Sua irmã é linda!
Read through the dialogue. Then listen again and follow along.
A
Nossa, Paulo, sua irmã é linda!
Wow, Paulo, your sister is beautiful!
B
Você acha? E o irmão dela? Lindo também, não é?
You think so? And her brother? Good looking too, right?
A
Você tem outro irmão?
You have another brother?
B
Não, só eu!
No, it's just me!
📌 Possessives: "sua" vs. "dela/dele"
In Brazilian Portuguese, possessives can be expressed two ways. Sua/seu can be ambiguous (your? his? her?), so Brazilians often prefer dela/dele (literally "of her/of him") for clarity:
MeaningOption 1 (possessive)Option 2 (preferred in speech)
your sistersua irmãa irmã de você / sua irmã ✓
her brotherseu irmão ⚠️ ambiguouso irmão dela ✓
his sistersua irmã ⚠️ ambiguousa irmã dele ✓
their housesua casa ⚠️ ambiguousa casa deles/delas ✓
Notice the dialogue uses both: "sua irmã" (clear — talking to Paulo) and "o irmão dela" (avoids ambiguity about whose brother).
📌 Describing people with SER + adjective
Adjectives in Portuguese agree in gender and number with the noun:
AdjectiveMasculineFeminine
beautiful / handsomelindolinda
tallaltoalta
youngjovemjovem
smartinteligenteinteligente
friendlysimpáticosimpática
Pattern: Ela é linda. / Ele é lindo. / Elas são lindas. / Eles são lindos.
🗣️ Ponto fonológico — "Nossa!": Short for Nossa Senhora! (Our Lady!), this is one of Brazil's most versatile exclamations — surprise, admiration, disbelief. Stress falls on the first syllable: NOS-sa. The double "s" gives it a soft hissing sound, nothing like the English "z" in "his." Practice: Nossa! Nossa! Nossa!
😄 Elogios no Brasil: Brazilians are generally warm and direct with compliments — saying someone is linda/lindo is natural and not considered forward. The expression "Você acha?" (You think so? / Really?) is a classic way to express pleasant surprise or fish for more compliments. And "Só eu!" (It's just me! / Only me!) is a punchline structure used for humorous self-reference — very Brazilian in its playful self-confidence.
