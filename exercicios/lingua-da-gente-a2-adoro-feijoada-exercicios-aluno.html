Read the dialogue

Two friends are having lunch. One of them is serving herself feijoada — and clearly loving it.

A: Você gosta mesmo de feijoada, não é?

You really like feijoada, don't you?

B: Adoro, principalmente nessa época do ano.

I love it, especially this time of year.

A: Mas você não acha que é muito pesada?

But don't you think it's kind of heavy?

B: Um pouco, por isso que eu coloco com umas fatias de laranja.

A little — that's why I have it with some orange slices.

A: Eu também não resisto, e me dá um pouco mais dessa farofa aí vai.

I can't resist it either, and give me a little more of that farofa, will you.

Language focus
Focus 1
Gostar de · Adorar — expressing likes
English: to like / to love
Gostar de means "to like" and always needs de before the noun or verb. Adorar means "to love / adore" and is used without preposition. Adding mesmo after the verb intensifies it: gosta mesmo = really likes.

Both follow the regular conjugation pattern for -ar verbs, but gostar is followed by de, not a direct object.
Você gosta de feijoada? — Do you like feijoada?
Note: gostar de + noun/infinitive — the de is required. Ela gosta muito de farofa.
She really likes farofa. Eu adoro feijoada, principalmente no inverno.
I love feijoada, especially in winter. Você gosta mesmo de cozinhar?
Do you really like to cook?
Focus 2
Achar que · Por isso que
English: to think that / that's why
Achar que = "to think that" — used to express opinions softly. It's much more common in everyday speech than pensar que.

Por isso que = "that's why / that's the reason why" — links a cause to its consequence. Equivalent to por essa razão, but far more colloquial and natural.
Você não acha que é muito pesada?
Don't you think it's too heavy? Eu acho que a feijoada é deliciosa.
I think feijoada is delicious. Por isso que eu coloco laranja.
That's why I put orange with it. É pesada, por isso que eu não como todo dia.
It's heavy, that's why I don't eat it every day.
Focus 3
Expressões e vocabulário da feijoada
Expressions & feijoada vocabulary
The dialogue packs in several essential expressions alongside the vocabulary of Brazil's most iconic dish.
Não resistir — can't resist / to be unable to say no
Não é? — Right? / Don't you think? (tag question)
Principalmente — especially / mainly / above all
Nessa época do ano — this time of year
Me dá… — Give me… (informal imperative, very natural)
Aí vai — there you go / OK then

Feijoada — black bean stew with pork cuts (Brazil's national dish)
Fatias de laranja — orange slices (served alongside to cut the richness)
Farofa — toasted manioc flour, a classic feijoada side
Pesada — heavy (rich, hard to digest)
🫘 Culture note Feijoada is considered Brazil's national dish. Traditionally served on Saturdays, it is a slow-cooked black bean stew with sausage, pork ribs, and other cuts, served with rice, couve (collard greens), farofa, and orange slices. The orange is not just decoration — the acidity helps digest the fat. It's a social meal: unhurried, abundant, and shared.
🔊 Phonology note Notice principalmente — 6 syllables: prin-ci-pal-men-te. Stress falls on the antepenultimate syllable. Similarly: laranja (la-ran-ja), farofa (fa-ro-fa). In BP, unstressed e at the end of a word is often reduced to a near-silent "i" sound: principalmente → /prinsipal'mẽtʃi/.

Exercises
Exercise 1 — Comprehension check
Choose the correct answer based on the dialogue.
Exercise 2 — Gostar de or adorar?
Fill in the gap with the correct form of gostar de or adorar. Pay attention to the preposition de after gostar.
1. Eu feijoada. Não resisto!
Dica: use a forma mais intensa (love it)
2. Você comida brasileira?
Dica: use gostar de (do you like…?)
3. Ela farofa, principalmente com feijoada.
Dica: use adorar (3ª pessoa)
4. Nós comer juntos no sábado.
Dica: use gostar de + infinitivo
5. Eles feijoada — pedem toda semana.
Dica: use a forma mais intensa (3ª pessoa plural)
Exercise 3 — Achar que or por isso que?
Choose acho que or por isso que to complete each sentence logically.
1. A feijoada é pesada. eu sempre coloco laranja.
2. a feijoada é o prato mais brasileiro que existe.
3. Ela não gosta de carne. ela não come feijoada.
4. o sábado é o melhor dia para comer feijoada.
5. O restaurante é muito bom. está sempre cheio.
Exercise 4 — What is it?
Match each word or expression to its correct description.
Exercise 5 — Your turn
Think of a dish or food you love (or can't resist). Write 4–6 sentences in Portuguese describing: what you love, why, whether anyone thinks it's "heavy" or unhealthy, and what you eat or drink alongside it. Use gostar de or adorar, achar que, por isso que, and at least one expression from Focus 3.
