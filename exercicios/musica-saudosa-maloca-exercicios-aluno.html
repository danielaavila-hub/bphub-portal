Saudosa Maloca Adoniran Barbosa · samba paulista

A classic samba tells the story of a demolished home — and teaches you how Brazilians really speak. Popular register vs. standard Portuguese.
música registro popular × padrão São Paulo ~30 min
Contexto Cultural
Adoniran Barbosa (1910–1982) is the father of samba paulista — samba from São Paulo. In "Saudosa Maloca" (1951), a man tells the story of the maloca (improvised shack) he built with two friends, Mato Grosso and Joca — until the owner of the land had it demolished to build a tall building. This recording is by Os Originais do Samba.
Adoniran wrote his lyrics in the popular Portuguese of working-class São Paulo — on purpose. Forms like nóis, as ferramenta and véia are not "mistakes" in the song: they are part of his art and identity. In this activity you will learn to recognize them and connect them to the standard forms you use in writing.
💬 The song made a Brazilian proverb famous: "Deus dá o frio conforme o cobertor" — literally, "God gives the cold according to the blanket": life only gives you what you can bear.
Ouça a música
First listen: just enjoy the story. Second listen: pay attention to the words that sound different from "textbook" Portuguese.

Exercise 1 · Compreensão

Verdadeiro ou falso?

Listen to the song and mark V (verdadeiro) or F (falso).

a) Antes do edifício alto, havia uma casa velha naquele lugar.
b) O narrador construiu a maloca sozinho.
c) O dono do terreno mandou derrubar a maloca.
d) Os amigos brigaram com os homens das ferramentas.
e) O narrador sente saudade da maloca.

Exercise 5 · Produção

Um lugar que deixa saudade

Think of a place from your life that no longer exists or that you miss — a house, a school, a café, a city. Write 4–6 sentences about it in Portuguese.

Use these ideas:
• Eu sinto saudade de… (I miss…)
• O lugar era… (The place was…)
• Lá tinha… (There was/were…)
• Eu morava / estudava / ia lá… (I used to live / study / go there…)
