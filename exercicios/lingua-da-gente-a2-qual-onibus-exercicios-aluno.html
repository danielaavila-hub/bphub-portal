Qual Ônibus Pegar?

Asking for directions on public transport in Brazil — prepositions of route, modal poder, and navigating São Paulo's bus system.

Read the dialogue

Two people at a bus stop in São Paulo. A is looking for the bus that goes to Rua Francisco Morato.

A: Com licença, esse vai pela Morato?

B: Não, esse vai pela Avenida Vital Brasil, até o Instituto Butantã.

A: Tá. Você sabe qual que eu devia pegar?

B: Pela Francisco Morato? Pode pegar o 702C. Ele passa aqui mesmo nessa parada.

A: Tá bom, obrigado.

A: Excuse me, does this one go down Morato?

B: No, this one goes down Avenida Vital Brasil, until the Butantã Institute.

A: OK. Do you know which one I should take?

B: Along Francisco Morato? You can take the 702C. It stops right here at this bus stop.

A: OK, good, thanks.

3
Language focus
Focus 1
Preposições de rota — pela / pelo / até
English: along / down / through / until
Pela / pelo = por + a/o. Use it to say which street or avenue a vehicle travels along. Até says where the journey ends.
Esse ônibus vai pela Avenida Paulista.
This bus goes along Avenida Paulista. O metrô passa pelo centro.
The metro goes through downtown. O 702C vai até o Instituto Butantã.
The 702C goes all the way to the Butantã Institute. Esse ônibus vai pela Francisco Morato até o terminal.
This bus goes down Francisco Morato all the way to the terminal.
Focus 2
Poder + infinitivo — suggestions & possibilities
English: you can / could take
In Brazilian Portuguese, pode + infinitive is the natural way to give directions or suggest an option. It is warmer and less blunt than a command.
Pode pegar o 702C.
You can take the 702C. Pode ir de metrô — é mais rápido.
You can go by metro — it's faster. Pode descer na próxima parada.
You can get off at the next stop.
Focus 3
Expressões-chave do diálogo
Key phrases for asking about transport
These expressions are ready to use in any city, at any bus stop.
Com licença — polite opener; gets someone's attention
Esse vai pela...? — Does this one go down...?
Qual que eu devia pegar? — Which one should I take? (informal spoken form)
Aqui mesmo — right here (mesmo = emphasis, not "same")
Parada — bus stop
Tá bom — OK, got it, sounds good
🏙 Culture note São Paulo has over 13,000 bus lines — the largest network in Brazil. Each line has a code like 702C. Finding the right bus is a daily challenge even for locals. Apps like SPTrans and Google Maps are widely used, but asking someone at the stop still works perfectly.
🔊 Phonology note The R in Brazilian Portuguese has two sounds: at the start of a word or doubled (rr), it sounds like English "h" — rua = "hua". Between vowels, it is a quick tap, like the "dd" in "ladder" — parada, pegar.
