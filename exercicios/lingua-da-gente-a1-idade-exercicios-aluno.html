💬 Dialogue

Quantos anos você tem?
Read through the dialogue. Then listen again and follow along.
A
Quantos anos você tem, Paulo?
How old are you, Paulo?
B
Adivinha.
Take a guess.
A
27 ou 29, por aí.
Around 27 or 29.
B
Acertou, estou com 27 anos.
You got it — I'm 27 years old.
📌 TER vs. ESTAR COM for age
In Brazilian Portuguese there are two ways to say your age:
FormExampleNote
TER + anosQuantos anos você tem?Most common in questions
ESTAR COM + anosEstou com 27 anos.Very natural in speech
Both are correct. Estar com literally means "to be with" — Brazilians "are with" their years, not "have" them!
🗣️ Ponto fonológico — "tô" vs. "estou": In informal speech, Brazilians almost always shorten estou → tô. So "Estou com 27 anos" sounds like "Tô com 27 anos." You'll hear this reduction constantly — with all estar forms: está → tá, estamos → tamo, estão → tão.
🎂 Idade no Brasil: Brazilians tend to be more open about age than people in some other cultures — asking someone's age is generally quite natural, especially among friends. The phrase "Adivinha!" (take a guess!) is a playful, very common way to dodge the question. You'll also hear "por aí" a lot — it means "around / about" and is used constantly for approximate numbers, places, and ideas.
